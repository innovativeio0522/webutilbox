What Is the Image Watermark Tool?

This tool is a browser-based watermarking utility that overlays text or image watermarks onto your photos using the HTML5 Canvas API. You can add a copyright notice, your name, your website URL, or a logo image to any photo, with full control over where the watermark appears, how large it is, and how transparent it looks. The watermark is permanently baked into the downloaded image, making it part of the visual content rather than a removable layer.

The tool supports two types of watermarks: text watermarks and image watermarks. Text watermarks let you type any text — such as "© Your Name 2024" or your website URL — and customize the font size, color, and opacity. Image watermarks let you upload a logo or signature image and overlay it on your photo at a specified size and position. Both types support nine preset positions (corners, edges, and center) as well as free drag-and-drop positioning for precise placement anywhere on the image.

Watermarking is an essential practice for photographers, artists, content creators, and businesses who share images online. A well-placed watermark clearly identifies you as the creator, deters unauthorized use and redistribution, and turns every shared image into a passive marketing opportunity by displaying your brand or website URL to everyone who sees it. This tool makes professional watermarking accessible to everyone, without requiring expensive software or technical expertise.

How to Use the Image Watermark Tool

Adding a watermark to your image is straightforward — follow these steps to get started.

  • Step 1: Upload your image by clicking the upload area or dragging and dropping a file. Supported formats include JPG, PNG, and WebP.
  • Step 2: Choose your watermark type — click "Text" to add a text watermark, or "Image" to upload a logo or signature image as the watermark.
  • Step 3: For text watermarks, type your watermark text in the input field and adjust the font size and color. For image watermarks, upload your logo file and set the size percentage.
  • Step 4: Select a position from the 3×3 position grid, or drag the watermark directly on the canvas preview to place it exactly where you want it.
  • Step 5: Adjust the opacity slider to control how transparent the watermark appears, then click "Download Watermarked Image" to save the result.

Tips and Best Practices

The placement and opacity of your watermark significantly affects both its effectiveness and the visual appeal of the image. A watermark that is too small or too transparent can be easily cropped out or ignored, while one that is too large or too opaque distracts from the image content. For most use cases, a watermark at 40–60% opacity placed in a corner or along the bottom edge strikes the right balance between visibility and subtlety.

For maximum protection against unauthorized use, place your watermark over the most important or visually interesting part of the image rather than in a corner. A watermark in the corner can be easily cropped out, while one that overlaps the main subject of the image cannot be removed without damaging the image. For stock photography and high-value images, consider using a larger, more prominent watermark that covers a significant portion of the image.

When using an image watermark, use a PNG file with a transparent background so only the logo or signature is visible, not a white or colored rectangle around it. A transparent PNG watermark blends naturally with any background color or texture in the underlying image. If your logo does not have a transparent background, use an image editor to remove the background before uploading it as a watermark.
