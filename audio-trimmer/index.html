Audio Trimmer - Cut and Trim Audio Files Online

The Audio Trimmer lets you cut and trim audio files directly in your browser with precision and ease. Whether you need to remove silence from the start of a recording, extract a specific clip from a longer file, or cut out an unwanted section in the middle, this tool handles it all without requiring any software installation or file uploads to a server.

What Is the Audio Trimmer?

The Audio Trimmer is a browser-based audio editing tool that uses the Web Audio API to load, analyze, and export specific segments of audio files. You set a start time and an end time, and the tool extracts exactly that portion of the audio and exports it as a high-quality WAV file. The entire process happens locally in your browser, so your audio files never leave your device.

The tool includes a visual waveform display that renders the amplitude of your audio over time, giving you a clear picture of the audio content before you make your cuts. This is especially useful for identifying silence, speech segments, music sections, and other audio features at a glance. Combined with the precise time input fields, you can trim to the exact second or fraction of a second you need.

A preview feature lets you listen to your selected segment before committing to the export. This saves time and ensures you get exactly the clip you want on the first try, without having to download and re-upload multiple times to fine-tune your selection.

How to Use the Audio Trimmer

Trimming audio with this tool is straightforward and takes just a few steps from upload to download.

  • Step 1: Click the "Upload Audio File" button and select the audio file you want to trim from your device. Supported formats include MP3, WAV, OGG, and M4A.
  • Step 2: Once the file loads, the waveform visualization and audio player will appear. Listen to the full file to identify the section you want to keep.
  • Step 3: Enter the start time in seconds in the "Start Time" field. This is the point where your trimmed clip will begin.
  • Step 4: Enter the end time in seconds in the "End Time" field. The duration display will update to show the length of your selected segment.
  • Step 5: Click "Preview Trimmed" to listen to your selection. If it sounds right, click "Download Trimmed Audio" to export the clip as a WAV file.

Common Use Cases

Audio trimming is one of the most common audio editing tasks, with applications across many fields and hobbies.

  • Podcast editing — remove dead air, false starts, or filler content from the beginning and end of recorded episodes before publishing.
  • Ringtone creation — extract the catchiest or most recognizable part of a song to use as a custom ringtone on your phone.
  • Voice memo cleanup — trim silence and background noise from the start and end of voice recordings to make them cleaner and more professional.
  • Sample extraction — pull a specific musical phrase, sound effect, or spoken word clip from a longer recording for use in a project.
  • Content creation — extract audio clips from interviews, lectures, or recordings for use in social media posts, highlight reels, or presentations.
  • Music production — isolate a specific section of a reference track or recording for analysis, sampling, or use in a digital audio workstation.

Tips and Best Practices

For the cleanest cuts, use the waveform display to visually identify natural pause points or silence in the audio. Cutting at a point of silence or low amplitude produces a cleaner result than cutting in the middle of a sound, which can create an abrupt or jarring transition. Zoom in on the waveform if you need to identify a precise cut point.

When creating ringtones or short clips, aim for a segment that starts and ends cleanly without abrupt cuts. If the audio has a natural fade-in or fade-out, try to include those in your selection for a more polished result. For speech recordings, cut just after the last word ends and just before the first word begins to avoid clipping any syllables.

The output is saved as a WAV file, which is uncompressed and lossless. This is ideal for further editing or professional use. If you need a smaller file for sharing or uploading, you can convert the WAV to MP3 using a separate audio converter after downloading. WAV ensures you always have the highest quality version of your trimmed clip.
