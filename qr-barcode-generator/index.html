What Are QR Codes and Barcodes?

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a two-dimensional matrix barcode that can store a wide variety of data types — URLs, plain text, contact information, phone numbers, SMS messages, email addresses, and even WiFi network credentials. Smartphones can scan QR codes instantly using their built-in cameras, making them one of the most versatile and widely used data-sharing formats in the world.

A barcode is a one-dimensional representation of data using parallel lines of varying widths. Barcodes are the standard for product identification in retail, logistics, healthcare, and manufacturing. Common formats include CODE128 (general purpose), EAN13 (retail products in Europe), UPC (retail products in North America), and CODE39 (industrial and government applications).

This tool supports both formats with full customization options including size, colors, error correction levels, and barcode dimensions — giving you complete control over the appearance and functionality of your generated codes.

Tips and Best Practices

For QR codes that will be printed at small sizes (such as on business cards), use a higher error correction level like "High" (30%). This makes the code more resilient to damage, smudging, or partial obstruction while still being scannable. For digital-only use where the code will always be displayed cleanly, "Medium" (15%) is a good balance between data density and reliability.

When customizing QR code colors, ensure there is sufficient contrast between the foreground and background colors. Dark foreground on light background is the most reliable combination. Avoid using similar shades or low-contrast color pairs, as these can cause scanning failures on some devices. Always test your custom-colored QR code with multiple devices before printing or publishing.

For barcodes, make sure your data matches the requirements of the selected format. EAN13 requires exactly 13 digits, UPC requires 12 digits, and ITF14 requires 14 digits. CODE128 is the most flexible format and accepts any combination of letters, numbers, and symbols, making it the best choice when you're unsure which format to use.
