What Is a URL Slug?

A URL slug is the human-readable portion of a web address that identifies a specific page. For example, in the URL https://example.com/how-to-bake-bread, the slug is how-to-bake-bread. Slugs replace spaces with hyphens, strip special characters, and use lowercase letters to create clean, readable links.

Search engines like Google use slugs as a ranking signal. A descriptive, keyword-rich slug tells both users and crawlers what a page is about before they even visit it. A well-crafted slug can improve click-through rates from search results and make your URLs easier to share and remember.

Poorly formatted URLs — full of percent-encoded characters, underscores, or random strings — hurt user experience and can negatively impact SEO. This tool ensures every slug you generate follows best practices automatically.

Tips and Best Practices

Keep your slugs short and focused. Aim for 3–5 meaningful words that capture the core topic of the page. Avoid stop words like "a", "the", "and", or "of" unless they are essential to the meaning. Shorter slugs are easier to share, remember, and type manually.

Always include your primary keyword in the slug. If your article is about "best running shoes for beginners," a slug like best-running-shoes-beginners is ideal. This signals relevance to search engines and improves organic ranking potential for that keyword.

Avoid changing slugs after a page is published. If you must change a slug, set up a 301 redirect from the old URL to the new one to preserve SEO value and prevent broken links. Consistency in URL structure also helps users navigate your site more intuitively.
